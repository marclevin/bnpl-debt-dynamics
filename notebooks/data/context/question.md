# Thesis Framework: BNPL & Credit Saturation in SA

## 1. Problem Statement

The SA consumer credit market is reaching a saturation point. BNPL enters this market
using alternative scoring, allowing debt accumulation that is 'invisible' to traditional
lenders. Does this expansion provide liquidity or accelerate systemic default?

## 2. Agent Archetypes (The Buckets) To be refined

* **Type A: The Unscorable Entry-Level:** No credit history, uses BNPL as their first 'gate.'
* **Type B: The Stressed Middle Class:** High DSTI (~35-40%), uses BNPL to bridge month-end.
* **Type C: The Strategic Optimizer:** Uses BNPL for 0% interest while keeping cash in savings.

## 3. Calibration Targets (The 'Reality' Benchmarks) To be refined

* **DSTI Ratio:** Target 30% - 50% based on NCR/Eighty20 2025 Stress Reports.
* **Default Rates:** Match the current ~34% arrears rate in the SA unsecured market.
* **BNPL Growth:** Model a market spend scaling toward R25 billion (SA 2026 estimate).

## 4. Primary Simulation Experiment

Introduce a "BNPL Shock" into a stable credit environment. 
Observe the shift in the 'Debt-to-Earnings' curve across 60 monthly ticks (5 years).
